Key Constitutional Articles

The Mexican Constitution of 1917 is one of the most comprehensive constitutions in the world. While it contains 136 articles, certain articles are particularly significant because they establish fundamental rights, define the structure of government, or address critical aspects of Mexican sovereignty and society.

This reference table highlights the most important constitutional articles commonly tested on the naturalization exam. Understanding these articles provides insight into Mexico's legal framework, citizen rights, and the principles that guide the nation.

Article Topic / Synopsis
1 Human rights protection for all.
2 Rights of Indigenous peoples and communities.
3 Right to secular, free, compulsory education.
4 Equality before the law; family protection.
5 Freedom to choose profession or work.
6 Freedom of expression.
7 Freedom of the press.
8 Right to petition authorities.
9 Right of association and assembly.
14 No retroactive laws; due process.
16 Legal certainty: no arbitrary acts, warrants needed.
24 Freedom of religion.
27 National ownership of land, water, subsoil resources.
28 Prohibition of monopolies.
30 Defines Mexican nationality.
33 Rights of foreigners; no interference in politics.

40 Federal, democratic, representative republic.
41 Division of powers; electoral system.
49 Separation of Legislative, Executive, Judicial powers.
102 Attorney General and Public Prosecutor's Office.
105 Constitutional controversies and actions.
107 Regulates "Amparo" (constitutional protection).
115 Municipal autonomy and framework.

130 Historical separation of Church and State.

Study Tip: Articles 1-29 establish individual guarantees and fundamental rights. Articles 30-38 address nationality and citizenship. Articles 39-136 define the structure and functioning of the Mexican state.
